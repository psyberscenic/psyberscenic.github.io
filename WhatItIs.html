Current Projects

Consolidating the behemoth of my hdd.

I have a ton of music saved to my external hdd--mostly stuff written in Cubase. A ton of project files,
a ton of audio files, presets, etc...I'm slowly going through it all both to organize the hdd as well as to
salvage what I can from these project files as I will most likely be switching over to Reaper. What this means
for you is that I have a lot of sample packs, midi files, and project files that will be made available
whenever I get through it all.

It's quite a lot of work and will take a while to get through but, over time, you will slowly start to see
things added to the store that you will hopefully find some value in :).

Three Articles.

One of the articles has already been completed--I just need to spruce it up a bit with some images
and relavent links. The second article is an article on Cubase's RoomWorks and has already been uploaded
to Lurker. I just need to decide if I want to include it on the site and such. The final article is a
detailed look at Albino3 as it relates to synthesizing and PsyTrance. I have heard a lot of entry-level
producers as a lot of questions about how you do "X" and I hope to answer a lot of those questions, as best
as I can with that article.

For the first article:
 It is the first of many articles that discusses the aftermath of your first satisfying production and
 where all your music can take you!

For the second article:
 This is just a simple article detailing the very basics of Cubase's RoomWorks on Lurker.
 If you wish to contribute to Lurker, feel free to just join the fun!
 Alternatively, you can contact me and I will get you introduced and setup.

Compiling the "Health Grimoire".

I've been through long periods of shit health. In dealing with these situations, I have
developed a keen interest in health--overall, basic, human well-being. I plan on compiling
all the information I have found that I feel is really helpful in contributing to quality
of life and presenting it in an easily digestable format.

This particular project might take some time but it will definitely be worth every minute :).
